# Stressed Librarian


## Gameplay Mechanics

### Summary
You play as a librarian with extreme anxiety trying to survive a shift. You have to make sure that the bookshelves aren't empty while managing loud, annoying customers. Keep an eye at your heart rate, if the library gets too loud and your stress becomes unbearable, it's game over.

### Genre: 
Arcade Time Management

### Target audience: 
Players who enjoy fast paced arcade games

### Game flow:
- Start at reception desk
- Grab box of books
- Navigate to correct section in library
- Place books 
- Encouter loud or lost customers
- Resolve their issue to lower stress
- Repeat

### Look and feel
PSX style graphics, low poly models, pixelated post processing
Set in a modern library
Screen effects are based on amount of stress (red vignette, screen shake, etc)

### Gameplay
The goal is to get the highest score while the game gets harder.

### Mechanics
- You have a box and there is a shelve, the box type must match the correct shelve type (SCI-FI box goes into SCI-FI shelve)
- Shushing customers
- You can encounter lost customers, they will *'politely'* ask you where X-type bookshelves are located, you must bring the customer to the right shelve and, they give you stress as long as they're following you.
- Sprinting, saves you time but gives you more stress


## Story / Immserive design

### Story
You are a librarian with stress. You must keep the library sorted and quiet, or else you'll breakdown because of the unbearable stress.

### Game art/Audio 
- PSX style, pixelated post processing
- Mumbling customers with text saying what they want (2D/3D text?)
- Fog or pixelation to block far view

### UI
- Heart rate monitor/Pumping heart to represent stress level
- Minimal UI, use world environment instead of UI, like box/shelve labels, fading arrow leading the way to make the game easier 


## Level design
- One library
- Short tutorial to teach controls, stress and npc system
